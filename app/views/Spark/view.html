Sparkmuse, an Application Idea Community

Last updated July 12, 2010 - I want to create a community where techies can throw out their ideas and get quality feedback from a diverse community, find potential cofounders, brainstorm new directions, and track their progress.

Problem

There presently isn't a go-to site on the net for discussing new tech ideas. Whether they be web or mobile apps, B2B or B2C, or oriented towards social or gaming, you are left with two alternatives: real contacts or discussion boards lacking for the task. You can certainly get great value from real contacts, especially if you live in a booming hub, but you miss out on cross-polination as most meetups are among birds of a feather. An online discussion board can give you this insight, but their functionality doesn't invite brainstorming and collaboration after the initial discussion.

Solution

I want to create a community where techies can throw out their ideas and get quality feedback from a diverse community, find potential cofounders, brainstorm new directions, and track their progress.

The technology solution isn't as difficult or interesting as will be forming the community itself. I've seen many requests per day for reviews and feedback in various places around the net and I think those are the best leads with which to start. For these people, I will both offer my insight to their request and then invite them to a more formal place for them to get more of it. I cannot think of a better way to form a community for idea discussion than to track down the people who most want it. I think they would be personally invested in participating and strengthening the site.
